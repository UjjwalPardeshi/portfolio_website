About Me

I’m Ujjwal Surajkumar Pardeshi, a passionate machine learning (ML) enthusiast with a keen interest in exploring the potential of AI, deep learning, and computer vision. Currently pursuing my B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering with a specialization in IoT at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, I’m dedicated to understanding how intelligent algorithms can analyze data, make predictions, and solve real-world problems. I’ve worked on projects like MyVoice, which provides a voice for the mute using TensorFlow and OpenCV, and MetaShard, a decentralized finance (DeFi) solution combining blockchain technologies. As a Research Fellow at IEEE SRMIST, I have implemented GradCAM for analyzing MSTAR datasets and integrated pose estimation models using OpenPose and TensorFlow. My mission is to continue building solutions that combine the power of AI and ML to optimize and innovate across various fields. I am actively seeking new challenges and opportunities in this exciting landscape.

Gesture based authentication system

A gesture-based authentication system is a security mechanism that allows users to unlock devices or access services by performing specific gestures, such as drawing shapes or making hand movements. These gestures are typically detected using sensors like touchscreens, cameras, or motion sensors, and are compared to a stored pattern for verification. This method enhances security by relying on dynamic inputs, making it harder for unauthorized users to replicate the exact gesture, while also providing a more intuitive and user-friendly alternative to traditional passwords or PINs.
